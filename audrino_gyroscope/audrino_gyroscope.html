Audrino Anklet

New York, NY

Step#1 - Initial Idea


I have always been interested in transferring music into graphics. The anklet is made to be a wearable device that enable people to have individual visual graphics based on the music they are listening on their phones. The visual element could be a new way to show your personality and a tool to indicate the status of listening music. It is a device that transfers people's physical movement into a new formate of visual graphics.

Step#2 - Generative Graphics


This could be a potential presentation of the generative graphics based on the audrino input.

Step#3 - Prototype


Audrino MPU6050 GY521 - 6 Axis Accelerometer + Gyro Sensor

Step#4 - Prototype


I started to work on how the actual product would look like and how I could assemble individual modules together while keep it fit the size of an anklet.

Step#5 - Second Prototype


I started to work on the material and connections between each component, including audrino board, wiring, and projector.

Step#5 - Final Documentation


The anklet is a product that using music and movement of user's ankle as input and provide the user with customized generative graphics. It is a digital wearable piece that aims to provide users with a new visual experience.

Step#6 - Future Iterantion


I've learned more about Audrino and the sensors through this project. For future iteration, I think I could use a sensor that better translate the degree and position of the ankle and then I could apply the data in a more precious way into the graphic design.
