Sets for Strangers - Landmark and Me

New York, NY

“Landmark and me” is a project that encourages one to explore the relationship between an architectural landmark and oneself. As one of the world-famous landmark, Flatiron has been a tourist spot that attracting tourists and photographers from all over the world. Flatiron Plaza, located at the north of the building to get the view that shows both the 5th Avenue and Broadway sides of the building. The Plaza has become the best location to photograph the building so it attracted tourists to stay around and take photos.

However, taking photos seems like the only activity that tourists can do. Tourists take photos and the building is photoed, what are the other relationships that we could create to link the inanimate building with animate individuals, to make the trip more valuable for tourist, to create more intimacy between the history of flatiron and individual experience, to create more memories for tourists who travel from far-away places.

First iteration: Free canvas


Description: We started with putting a empty canvas on the Flatiron Plaza. One side is sketch board and another side is scratch board.

Outcomes:
・Crowd engaged in sketching
・People follow the trend from previous sketches
・It was too obvious interaction for many

Second Iternation: Express yourself with stickers

Description: The second iteration involves giving certain constraints in the form of words to the audience so that they can share their experiences with Flatiron in the form of sentences.

Outcomes:
・Not many interacted
・Too much constraint on words
・Limited choices
・Complex mechanism

Final Iteration: Drawing on the little Flatiron

Description: For the final iteration, we made a flatiron building model and gained lots of attention from tourists because of the form. People who participated into the drawing are very creative, including comic book stories, graphic patterns, group signatures, and holiday symbols.

Outcomes:
・Many interacted
・Lots of creativity
・Tourist took photos with our model
・Simple mechanism
